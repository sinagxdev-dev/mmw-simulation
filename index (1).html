SYMMETRY

Patterns and Numbers in Nature — TEDx NUFV

Symmetry is a sense of harmonious proportion — balance. An object has symmetry when it remains unchanged under a transformation: a reflection, a rotation, or a change in scale. Bilateral symmetry — the left and right sides are mirror images along a central midline, like a fish. Radial symmetry — the body is arranged around a central point, divisible into equal halves through multiple planes, like a sea star.
— Live Simulation
Bilateral Symmetry
One axis divides the body into two mirror halves. Found in fish, dolphins, sharks, and humans. Enables efficient directional movement — both sides generate equal thrust and drag.
Radial Symmetry
Body radiates equally from a central point through multiple planes. Found in sea stars, jellyfish, and sea anemones. Suits stationary or drifting animals that need to sense all directions equally.
Ecosystem Balance
In a coral reef, symmetry operates at scale. Remove one species and the system tilts. A healthy reef — like a symmetric body — depends on every part being in balance with the rest.

FRACTALS

Patterns and Numbers in Nature — TEDx NUFV

A fractal is a curve or geometric figure where each part has the same statistical character as the whole. The key property is self-similarity — zoom into any section and it looks like the entire shape at a smaller scale. Fractals have infinite detail at every level of magnification. Coined by Benoît Mandelbrot in 1975, but nature had been producing them for billions of years before we had a word for them.

Spirals

Patterns and Numbers in Nature — TEDx NUFV

A spiral is a self-similar curved pattern that expands outward from a center point at a constant rate per revolution. The logarithmic spiral is governed by the Golden Ratio — φ ≈ 1.618 — which appears in the Fibonacci sequence: 1, 1, 2, 3, 5, 8, 13… each term approximately 1.618× the previous. Critical distinction: a spiral is not a circle. A circle returns to the exact same point. A spiral returns to the same direction — but always further out. It is mathematically impossible for a logarithmic spiral to close on itself.

ANIMAL PATTERNS

Patterns and Numbers in Nature — TEDx NUFV

Turing's Reaction-Diffusion System (1952) describes how two chemicals — an activator and an inhibitor — interact across a developing embryo's surface. The activator promotes pigment; the inhibitor suppresses it. Because they diffuse at different rates, zones of high and low pigment form. Change just two parameters — the diffusion rates — and the output shifts from stripes → spots → blotches. Same equation. Different numbers. Completely different pattern.

TESSELLATION

Patterns and Numbers in Nature — TEDx NUFV

Tessellation is a repeating pattern of polygons that covers a flat surface with no gaps and no overlaps. For a polygon to tessellate, its interior angles must sum to exactly 360° at every vertex. Of all regular polygons, only three can tile a flat plane on their own: the equilateral triangle, the square, and the regular hexagon. The hexagon is the most efficient — it encloses the greatest area for the least perimeter. Nature always optimizes. The hexagon is the optimal solution.

Giant's Causeway
When lava cools and contracts, stress fractures form. The geometry minimizing total crack length while covering the surface completely is the hexagon — thousands of hexagonal basalt columns, produced by physics alone.
Honeycombs & Dry Soil
Bees instinctively build hexagonal cells — the geometry that stores the most honey for the least wax. Cracked dry soil forms Voronoi-like polygon tessellations as moisture evaporates and the surface contracts.
Efficiency Proof
For a fixed perimeter: Hexagon encloses ≈2.598 sq units · Square encloses 2.25 sq units · Triangle encloses ≈1.732 sq units. The hexagon wins — same boundary, most space. Nature converges on this independently, across materials.

The human body exhibits bilateral symmetry — two arms, two legs, two eyes, two lungs, two cerebral hemispheres, mirror images along the central anatomical midline. Beyond structure, the body demonstrates mathematical proportion: the Golden Ratio φ ≈ 1.618 appears measurably throughout — total height to navel height, forearm to hand, shoulder-to-fingertip to elbow-to-fingertip. The same constant that governs galaxy arms governs your own body.
